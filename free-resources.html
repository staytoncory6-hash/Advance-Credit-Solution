Credit Cleanup Checklist

Use this checklist as a roadmap before sending any disputes. Don‚Äôt rush ‚Äî being organized gives you power.

  1. Pull all three credit reports (Equifax, Experian, TransUnion) as close to the same date as possible.
  2. Highlight every negative item: collections, charge-offs, late payments, repossessions, public records.
  3. Write the creditor name, account number (or partial), balance, and important dates for each item.
  4. Mark anything that looks suspicious: wrong dates, wrong amounts, mixed names, or accounts that are ‚Äúnot mine.‚Äù
  5. Group similar issues together (all medical, all old collections, all late payments, etc.).
  6. Choose the easiest or most obviously inaccurate items to dispute first to build momentum.
  7. Create a folder (physical or digital) for letters, reports, envelopes, and responses.
  8. Track mailing dates, certified mail numbers, and response deadlines in one simple log.
  9. Make notes on every response you receive so you can see the story of your cleanup over time.

Sample Dispute Letter Pack

These are example paragraphs and structures. Always customize with your own information and truth. Keep copies of everything you send and everything they send back.

1. General Inaccuracy Dispute

To Whom It May Concern:

I am writing to dispute the accuracy of information on my credit report. Please investigate
the items listed below and remove or correct any information that cannot be verified as
complete and accurate.

[Insert account name, account number (if available), and what is wrong]

This information does not reflect my true payment history and appears to be reported in error.
Under the Fair Credit Reporting Act, I am requesting that you conduct a reasonable investigation
and provide me with written results of your findings.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Last 4 of SSN]
[Date of Birth]

2. Late Payment Date Dispute

To Whom It May Concern:

I am disputing the late payment reporting for the account listed below:

[Insert account name, account number (if available), and reported late date]

Your report shows a late payment that is not accurate based on my records. Please review your
records and correct any late payment information that cannot be verified as accurate. If you
cannot verify the late payment, please remove it from my credit file.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Last 4 of SSN]
[Date of Birth]

3. Fraudulent / Not Mine Account

To Whom It May Concern:

I am writing to dispute a fraudulent account appearing on my credit report. I did not open
this account and I am not responsible for it.

[Insert account name, account number (if available), and how it is being reported]

Please block this account from my file and remove any associated negative information. I am
requesting that you investigate this as potential identity theft and send me the results in
writing. I am also keeping copies of this dispute for my records.

Sincerely,
[Your Name]
[Your Address]
[Last 4 of SSN]
