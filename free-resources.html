Where Do I Even Start With Disputes?

If you’ve never done a dispute before, it can feel overwhelming. Here’s a simple, honest way to think about it:

  1. Pull all three reports: Experian, Equifax, and TransUnion. You can use AnnualCreditReport.com or a monitoring service.
  2. Highlight the problems: late payments, collections, charge-offs, bankruptcies, wrong personal info, duplicated accounts, etc.
  3. Decide what to dispute first: focus on things that are inaccurate, incomplete, or can’t be verified — not just “things you don’t like.”
  4. Write it down clearly: who the creditor is, what’s wrong, and what you want fixed (corrected, deleted, updated).
  5. Send it in writing: letter, proof, and a copy of the report with the items highlighted.

This is just the beginning. Our Elite Membership goes much deeper with step-by-step guidance, advanced letters, and proper mailing etiquette that can make all the difference.
