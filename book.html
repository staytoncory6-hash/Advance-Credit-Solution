‚ÄúPiggybacking‚Äù is when you become an authorized user on someone else‚Äôs well-managed credit card account so that their good history shows up on your report too.

Identity theft is more than just ‚Äúsomeone used my card.‚Äù It can involve new accounts, loans, or even public records in your name. If you suspect identity theft, you need to treat it like an emergency ‚Äì but also stay organized.

Here‚Äôs a basic step-by-step plan you can adapt:

  1. Document what happened. Write down dates, companies, account numbers, and how you found out.
  2. File a police report. This creates an official record. Keep a copy.
  3. Place fraud alerts or credit freezes. Contact the bureaus to add protections.
  4. Dispute fraudulent accounts and inquiries. Send detailed letters with copies of your ID and police report.
  5. Check all three reports regularly. Look for any new weird activity.
  6. Keep a paper trail. Save every letter, response, and confirmation number.
